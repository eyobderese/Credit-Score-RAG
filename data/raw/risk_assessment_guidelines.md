## 1. Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio Standards

### 1.1 DTI Calculation Method

**Front-End DTI (Housing Ratio):**
```
Front-End DTI = (Monthly Housing Expenses) / (Gross Monthly Income)
```

Monthly Housing Expenses include:
- Principal and interest
- Property taxes
- Homeowner's insurance
- HOA fees
- Mortgage insurance (if applicable)

**Back-End DTI (Total Debt Ratio):**
```
Back-End DTI = (Total Monthly Debt Payments) / (Gross Monthly Income)
```

### 1.2 Maximum DTI Ratios by Product

**Conventional Mortgages:**
- Front-End DTI: Maximum 28%
- Back-End DTI: Maximum 36%
- Exception approval: Up to 43% with compensating factors

**FHA Mortgages:**
- Front-End DTI: Maximum 31%
- Back-End DTI: Maximum 43%
- Automated approval: Available up to 50% with credit score ≥680

**Personal Loans:**
- Back-End DTI: Maximum 43%
- Preferred: ≤36%

**Business Loans:**
- Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR): Minimum 1.25
- Personal DTI (for personal guarantee): Maximum 45%

**Auto Loans:**
- Payment-to-Income (PTI) Ratio: Maximum 15%
- Back-End DTI: Maximum 45%

### 1.3 DTI Compensating Factors

Applicants exceeding standard DTI may qualify with:
- Credit score ≥740: +5% DTI allowance
- Cash reserves ≥12 months: +5% DTI allowance
- Minimal increase in housing payment (<10%): +3% DTI allowance
- Significant non-taxable income: +4% DTI allowance

**Maximum combined allowance:** 10% above standard limits

## 2. Collateral Requirements

### 2.1 Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratios

**Primary Residence - Purchase:**
| Credit Score | Maximum LTV | Minimum Down Payment |
|--------------|-------------|---------------------|
| 740+ | 97% | 3% |
| 680-739 | 95% | 5% |
| 620-679 | 90% | 10% |
| <620 | 80% | 20% |

**Primary Residence - Refinance:**
- Credit score ≥740: Maximum 95% LTV
- Credit score 680-739: Maximum 90% LTV
- Credit score 620-679: Maximum 85% LTV
- Cash-out refinance: Maximum 80% LTV

**Investment Property:**
- Single unit: Maximum 75% LTV
- 2-4 units: Maximum 70% LTV
- Minimum credit score: 680
- Minimum reserves: 6 months PITI

**Commercial Property:**
- Owner-occupied: Maximum 80% LTV
- Non-owner occupied: Maximum 70% LTV
- Special use property: Maximum 65% LTV

### 2.2 Vehicle Collateral Standards

**Auto Loans:**
- New vehicles (current model year): Maximum 120% LTV
- New vehicles (prior model year): Maximum 110% LTV
- Used vehicles (2-4 years old): Maximum 110% LTV
- Used vehicles (5-7 years old): Maximum 100% LTV
- Used vehicles (8+ years old): Not eligible

**Required vehicle value verification:**
- NADA, KBB, or Edmunds average trade-in value
- Minimum of 2 valuation sources required
- Use lower of values if discrepancy >10%

## 3. Industry Risk Classifications

### 3.1 Low-Risk Industries (Standard Terms)

- Healthcare (established practices)
- Education
- Government contractors
- Utilities
- Professional services (legal, accounting)

**Benefits:**
- Standard underwriting criteria
- No additional reserves required
- Standard interest rates

### 3.2 Medium-Risk Industries (Enhanced Review)

- Retail
- Manufacturing
- Hospitality
- Transportation
- Technology services

**Requirements:**
- 3 months bank statements required
- Business history: Minimum 2 years
- Additional reserves: 3 months operating expenses

### 3.3 High-Risk Industries (Strict Requirements)

- Restaurants and bars
- Construction
- Oil and gas
- Real estate speculation
- Startups (<2 years operating history)

**Requirements:**
- Credit score: +20 points above standard
- DTI ratio: -5% from standard maximum
- Down payment: +10% above standard
- Additional reserves: 6 months operating expenses
- Interest rate: +1.5% to +2.5% above base rate
- Personal guarantee required for business loans

### 3.4 Prohibited Industries

The following industries are not eligible for financing:
- Cannabis (where federally illegal)
- Adult entertainment
- Gambling operations
- Payday lending
- Speculative cryptocurrency ventures

## 4. Income Verification Standards

### 4.1 W-2 Employees

**Standard Documentation:**
- Most recent 2 years W-2 forms
- Most recent 30 days pay stubs
- Verbal or written employment verification

**Stability Requirements:**
- Current employer: Minimum 2 years
- OR same industry: Minimum 2 years with 30-day gap maximum
- Job change in last 6 months: Requires 60-day employment verification

**Income Calculation:**
- Base salary: Use current annual rate
- Overtime/bonus: Must have 2-year history, use average
- Commission: Must have 2-year history, use lesser of average or YTD trend

### 4.2 Self-Employed Borrowers

**Documentation Required:**
- Personal tax returns (2 years) with all schedules
- Business tax returns (2 years) if applicable
- Year-to-date profit & loss statement
- Business license (if applicable)
- CPA letter (loans >$250K)

**Income Calculation:**
- Use lesser of 2-year average or most recent year
- Add back: depreciation, depletion, casualty losses
- Subtract: one-time income items
- Declining income (>10% year-over-year): Use most recent year only

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Business history: 2 years minimum
- Credit score: +10 points above standard for same product
- Reserves: 6 months PITI or operating expenses

### 4.3 Non-Traditional Income

**Rental Income:**
- Use 75% of gross rental income
- Requires lease agreement and 2 years tax return documentation
- Negative rental history: Full amount counted against DTI

**Investment Income:**
- 2-year history required via tax returns
- Use lesser of 2-year average or current year
- Continuance: Must verify assets remain invested

**Social Security/Pension:**
- Must continue for at least 3 years
- Award letter or bank statements required
- Full amount may be used

**Alimony/Child Support:**
- Must be received for minimum 12 months
- Must continue for at least 3 years
- Court order or separation agreement required
- Proof of receipt (6 months bank statements)

## 5. Liquid Reserves Requirements

### 5.1 Reserve Calculation

**One Month Reserve Equals:**
- Principal + Interest
- Property taxes
- Insurance
- HOA fees
- Mortgage insurance

### 5.2 Minimum Reserves by Property Type

**Primary Residence:**
- 1 unit: 2 months
- 2 units: 3 months
- 3-4 units: 6 months

**Second Home:**
- Minimum: 2 months
- Preferred: 4 months

**Investment Property:**
- 1 property: 6 months
- 2 properties: 9 months
- 3+ properties: 12 months

**Business Loans:**
- Standard risk: 3 months operating expenses
- Medium risk: 6 months operating expenses
- High risk: 9 months operating expenses

### 5.3 Acceptable Reserve Assets

**100% Credit:**
- Checking/savings accounts
- Money market accounts
- Certificates of deposit (without early withdrawal penalty)
- Stocks, bonds, mutual funds (70% of value)

**Partial Credit:**
- Retirement accounts (60% of value after tax/penalty)
- Stocks subject to vesting (50% of vested value)
- Cash value life insurance (100% of cash surrender value)

**Not Acceptable:**
- Borrowed funds
- Unsecured credit lines
- Gift funds (unless properly documented and seasoned)
- Non-vested stock options

## 6. Geographic Risk Factors

### 6.1 Market Conditions

**Stable Markets (Tier 1):**
- Major metropolitan areas with diverse economies
- Population growth >2% annually
- Unemployment rate <5%
- Standard underwriting criteria apply

**Moderate Risk Markets (Tier 2):**
- Mid-size cities with stable employment
- Population growth 0-2% annually
- Unemployment rate 5-7%
- Additional scrutiny on appraisals
- LTV reduced by 5% for investment properties

**Declining Markets (Tier 3):**
- Single-industry dependent areas
- Population decline or stagnation
- Unemployment rate >7%
- LTV reduced by 10% across all products
- Additional reserves required (+2 months)
- Second homes/investment properties: Case-by-case approval

### 6.2 Disaster-Prone Areas

**Properties in FEMA Flood Zones:**
- Flood insurance required
- Additional reserves: +1 month
- Maximum LTV: 90% (primary residence), 75% (investment)

**Earthquake Zones:**
- Earthquake insurance required for loans >$500K
- Foundation inspection required for structures >25 years old

**Hurricane-Prone Coastal Areas:**
- Windstorm insurance required
- Property inspection required annually for investment properties
- Additional reserves: +2 months

## 7. Fraud Risk Indicators

### 7.1 Red Flags - Automatic Escalation

**Application Red Flags:**
- Address discrepancies across documents
- Recent name changes without documentation
- SSN issued in different state than claimed residence history
- Employment verification number matches applicant phone
- Multiple applications with different information

**Income Red Flags:**
- Pay stubs with calculation errors
- Employer not verifiable via public records
- Round numbers on pay stubs (no cents)
- Mismatched fonts on employment documents
- Tax returns without IRS tax transcript verification

**Asset Red Flags:**
- Bank statements without full account numbers
- Large recent deposits without sourcing
- Sequential statement dates but non-sequential balances
- Digital manipulation of statements
- Funds transferred between accounts to inflate reserves

### 7.2 Enhanced Due Diligence Requirements

When red flags identified:
1. Direct verification with third parties (mandatory)
2. IRS tax transcript (4506-C) required
3. Additional 2 months bank statements
4. Personal interview with underwriter
5. Management approval required
6. Minimum 15-day funding delay for verification

## 8. Exception Approval Authority

### 8.1 Authority Limits

**Senior Underwriter:**
- Single guideline variance
- Loan amount <$250,000
- Variance impact <5% of guideline

**Underwriting Manager:**
- Up to 2 guideline variances
- Loan amount <$500,000
- Variance impact <10% of guideline

**Chief Credit Officer:**
- Multiple variances
- Any loan amount
- Any variance level
- Final authority on all exceptions

### 8.2 Non-Delegable Requirements

The following CANNOT be excepted under any circumstances:
- Minimum credit score <550
- Active bankruptcy
- Fraud indicators
- Prohibited industries
- DTI >55% (any product)
- LTV >100% (any product)
